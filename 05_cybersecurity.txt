Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access or disruption. As more work, communication, and finance move online, digital security has become a basic requirement rather than a specialized concern. Individuals and organizations both face risks such as phishing, weak passwords, malware, and data leaks.

Simple habits can prevent many common problems. Using strong unique passwords, enabling multi-factor authentication, updating software, and avoiding suspicious links are among the most effective first steps. Security is often less about a single advanced tool and more about consistent attention to everyday practices.

For organizations, cybersecurity also involves policy, training, monitoring, and incident response. A secure environment depends on technical controls and human behavior working together. No system is perfect, but preparation reduces damage. Good security culture means people report issues early, follow procedures, and treat digital safety as a shared responsibility.
