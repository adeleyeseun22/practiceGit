Climate change describes long-term shifts in temperature, rainfall, and weather patterns, largely driven by human activity such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation. Its effects are visible in heatwaves, stronger storms, flooding, drought, and pressure on food and water systems. These changes do not affect all communities equally.

Some populations face greater exposure because of geography, poverty, weak infrastructure, or limited institutional support. Coastal communities may face erosion and rising seas, while dry regions may face crop failure and water shortages. Climate risk is therefore both an environmental issue and a development issue.

Responses to climate change include mitigation and adaptation. Mitigation aims to reduce emissions, while adaptation focuses on preparing for the effects already underway. Renewable energy, efficient transport, resilient agriculture, and better planning all have roles to play. Effective climate action requires both local solutions and wider policy commitment.
